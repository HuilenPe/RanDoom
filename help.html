We understand that exploring the Random Functionality feature in the RanDoom app may raise a few questions. Here are some helpful insights to assist you:

How does the Random Functionality work? ▼

How does the Random Functionality work? ▶︎

The Random Functionality generates a unique recipe suggestion for you with a single tap. It selects a recipe at random from our diverse collection, ensuring a delightful surprise each time.

How can I access the Random Functionality? ▼

How can I access the Random Functionality? ▶︎

Look for the "Random" button or icon within the app. Simply tap it to trigger the randomness and receive a recipe suggestion tailored to your preferences. Can I customize the randomness to suit my needs? Absolutely! We provide options to customize the randomness. You can apply filters based on meal type, cooking time, specific ingredients, or allergens. This way, you can maintain control while still enjoying the surprise factor.

What if I don't like the recipe generated by the Random Functionality? ▼

What if I don't like the recipe generated by the Random Functionality? ▶︎

Not every recipe may be to your liking, and that's perfectly okay! If the suggestion doesn't appeal to you, simply tap the "Random" button again for a new suggestion. Feel free to explore until you find a recipe that sparks your interest.

Can I save the recipe suggested by the Random Functionality? ▼

Can I save the recipe suggested by the Random Functionality? ▶︎

Absolutely! If a recipe captures your attention and you want to save it for future reference, you can easily send it to yourself via mail. This way, you can access it whenever you're ready to try it out.

Is the Random Functionality available for specific dietary preferences? ▼

Is the Random Functionality available for specific dietary preferences? ▶︎

Yes! The Random Functionality considers your selected dietary preferences and restrictions. It ensures that the generated recipe aligns with your chosen criteria, such as vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, or other preferences you've set within the app.
